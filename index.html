Gtasktic

A Google Tasks client written in HTML5 and packaged for Mac

Gtasktic is the first app I've written during my work with Taskstrike. It is backed by the local->cloud sync engine that is the core technology I've been working on for Taskstrike. It works offline and online, saving change to tasks you make while offline and then syncing them back again. It also implements real-time sync, where an action you make is synced to the cloud in real time and one time sync, where all the data is synced with the cloud at once.

ABOUT ME

I'm a Cornell CS grad. (07 Undergrad, 08 M.Eng.) I worked at Goldman for 2 years out of college before leaving to pursue my passion for startups. I read hacker news religiously. Tech stuff I'm interested in:

HTML5

It is far easier to build customized apps in HTML5 than any native architecture. The reason is HTML is a developed language that makes visual design extremely easy and other alternatives like ios has yet to catch up. HTML5 also has built in database through localstorage and indexdb.

The ability to package HTML5 as a app through webkit on the desktop and phonegap on mobile also makes it the most ubiquitous platform.

Cloud Data & Syncing

Most application data now are stored on the cloud, and working with that is crucial for any application. Cloud storage like Google Drive and Dropbox will probably take over in the future as people have multiple devices and need to sync their data across them.

Knowing how to build application that uses the dominant cloud providers will be key.


Replacing decision making with machine learning

Working at my current job with large databases and calculations on them, I am always interested in faster ways to do calculations on a dataset. Some approaches I have taken are using in memory tables or more efficient querying. I am also interested in applying machine learning to large sets of data to try to solve certain matching problems such as employers to jobs or dating.
